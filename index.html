Have you ever stumbled upon a YouTube channel that completely transformed your perspective on a subject? For me, that transformative moment occurred when I discovered "The Coding Train." This channel, hosted by Daniel Shiffman, is a treasure trove of coding tutorials and creative coding challenges and they ignited my passion for programming, also led me to explore more fascinating projects.

His videos primarily focused on p5.js, a JavaScript library aimed at making coding accessible and fun for creative individuals. I started with his "Introduction to p5.js" series, which provided a solid foundation for understanding the library's core concepts. Daniel's explanations were clear, and his coding challenges were not only educational but also incredibly entertaining.

Eventually I moved on to learn web development, got a full time job which is much more exciting than my previous job as a mechanical engineering.

Recently, I came accross a vidoe by ThePrimeagen on youtube, where he was creating the Conway's game of life (Wikipedia) using HTMX. The coding train also had a video on it and I decided to create that using p5.js. The animation running about is the canvas element of HTML, which is being drawn by p5.js.
